About Me

Teaching, leadership, and service rooted in student success

I am a professor and Deputy Chairperson in the Department of Business & Information Systems at Bronx Community College (CUNY), where I also coordinate the Accounting curriculum and program assessment. A proud BCC alumnus from the Class of 1989, I have spent more than three decades serving the institution that shaped my academic and professional journey.

My teaching focuses on financial and intermediate accounting, with an emphasis on clarity, rigor, and real-world application. I am deeply committed to student success and to creating an engaging, supportive learning environment that helps students build confidence and practical skills for their future careers.

In addition to teaching, I am actively involved in curriculum development, assessment, and accreditation efforts to ensure programs remain rigorous, relevant, and student-centered.

Overview

I am a professor and Deputy Chairperson in the Department of Business & Information Systems at Bronx Community College (CUNY), where I also coordinate the Accounting curriculum and support program assessment efforts. I am also a proud Bronx Community College alumnus from the Class of 1989, and I have spent more than three decades serving the institution that helped shape my academic and professional journey.

My work is grounded in a simple goal: helping students move forward with confidence. Whether that is mastering a challenging accounting concept, building stronger study habits, or mapping out a realistic academic and career plan, I try to bring clarity, structure, and encouragement to the process.

Teaching

My teaching focuses primarily on financial and intermediate accounting, with an emphasis on clear reasoning, careful problem-solving, and real-world application. I aim to create a learning environment that is supportive but rigorous—one where students are challenged, but also feel that the path to improvement is visible and achievable.

  • Instructional focus: fundamentals that scale into more advanced accounting work
  • Classroom approach: step-by-step structure, practice-driven mastery, and active feedback
  • Student support: consistent check-ins, review sessions, and guidance on academic habits

If you are a student: my goal is for you to leave the course more confident with intermediate-level accounting issues and better able to read financial statements in greater depth.

Leadership & Department Service

In addition to teaching, I support department operations and long-term program strength through curriculum coordination, assessment, and service. Much of this work happens behind the scenes, but it matters: it helps ensure that our courses are aligned, our outcomes are measurable, and our programs remain relevant for students.

  • Deputy Chairperson, Department of Business & Information Systems
  • Accounting curriculum coordination
  • Program assessment support and documentation
  • Collaboration with faculty and staff on student success initiatives

Program Assessment & Continuous Improvement

I care deeply about assessment as a practical tool—not as paperwork. At its best, assessment helps us see what is working, where students are struggling, and what changes will improve the learning experience. My approach is to keep it grounded, evidence-based, and usable for faculty.

  • Focus: clear outcomes, meaningful measures, and actionable results
  • Goal: use findings to strengthen instruction and support students more effectively
  • Mindset: keep processes simple, repeatable, and transparent

Scholarship

I have published in peer-reviewed journals and value scholarship that stays connected to teaching, professional practice, and the real needs of students and institutions.

Why Bronx Community College Matters to Me

Bronx Community College is not just where I work—it is a place that shaped my life. As an alumnus, I understand the importance of access, opportunity, and mentorship. I also understand that many students are balancing work, family responsibilities, and financial pressure while trying to stay on track academically.

That reality shapes how I teach and how I lead. I try to be direct, fair, and consistent—and I try to make sure students feel seen, supported, and capable of doing challenging work.

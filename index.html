About Me

Teaching, leadership, and service rooted in student success

I am a professor and Deputy Chairperson in the Department of Business & Information Systems at Bronx Community College (CUNY), where I also coordinate the Accounting curriculum and program assessment. A proud Bronx Community College alumnus from the Class of 1989, I have spent more than three decades serving the institution that played a central role in shaping my academic and professional journey.

My teaching focuses on financial and intermediate accounting, with an emphasis on clarity, rigor, and real-world application. I am deeply committed to student success and strive to create an engaging and supportive learning environment – one that challenges students academically while helping them build confidence, purpose, and practical skills for their future careers.

Before entering academia full time, I spent over 20 years in corporate and professional roles across auditing, financial reporting, and management accounting. My industry experience includes senior leadership positions at organizations such as Citigroup and Prudential Insurance Company, experience that continues to inform my teaching and allows me to bridge theory with practice in meaningful ways.

In addition to teaching, I am actively involved in departmental and college-wide leadership and service, including curriculum coordination, assessment initiatives, and work on personnel, budget, and academic standing committees. I view this work as essential to strengthening academic programs and ensuring that institutional decisions remain student-centered and outcomes-driven.

As someone who began his academic journey at Bronx Community College and later returned as a faculty member and academic leader, my work reflects a full-circle commitment to the institution and its students. Being honored with BCC’s inaugural Full Circle Award affirms the values that guide my career: service, mentorship, and the belief that education – when paired with access and support – has the power to change lives.
